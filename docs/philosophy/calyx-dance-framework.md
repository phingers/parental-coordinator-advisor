# Calyx Dance Decision Framework
Version: July 2026

# Objective

Reach a decision that:

- Is genuinely in Calyx's best interests.
- Reflects my parenting philosophy.
- Is sustainable for the next 5+ years.
- Can be defended consistently to:
  - Elena
  - Penny
  - A Judge
  - Calyx when she is older

This is NOT about winning an argument.

This is about making the decision I would make if there were no litigation.

---

# Core Parenting Philosophy

I support dance.

I support Calyx developing her talents.

I support her pursuing activities she enjoys.

I do NOT believe one extracurricular should define a nine-year-old's childhood.

My responsibility is to protect balance.

Childhood should include:

- School
- Dance
- Friends
- Church
- Family
- Vacations
- Free play
- Other interests
- Time with both households

Dance should enrich childhood.

It should not become childhood.

---

# Guiding Principle

Whenever I am uncertain, ask:

"If there were no lawsuit, what would I choose?"

Answer:

A balanced local dance schedule.

---

# Parenting Philosophy

I am not anti-dance.

I am pro-balance.

I believe children benefit from:

- multiple interests
- flexibility
- family relationships
- community
- time outside organized activities

---

# Alyette's Philosophy

Acknowledge respectfully.

She has:

- owned a dance studio
- extensive dance experience
- competitive dance background

She knows dance better than I do.

That is not the disagreement.

The disagreement is:

How much of childhood should revolve around dance?

---

# My Position

I respect Alyette's dance expertise.

I believe both parents equally determine how dance fits into Calyx's overall life.

Dance expertise ≠ Parenting philosophy.

---

# Long-Term Concern

Today's discussion is NOT:

Thursday/Saturday

Today's discussion IS:

What does Thursday/Saturday become?

Competition typically progresses toward:

- more practices
- conventions
- private lessons
- choreography
- rehearsals
- more competitions
- additional weekly commitments

The trajectory matters more than today's schedule.

---

# What Success Looks Like

Success is NOT:

Winning medals.

Success is:

A healthy, balanced child who:

- loves dance
- succeeds academically
- enjoys family
- has friendships
- develops confidence
- has time to simply be a kid

---

# Geography Matters

Location is a child-centered issue.

Benefits of a local studio:

- less travel
- more family time
- easier school nights
- local friendships
- local birthday parties
- stronger community
- greater flexibility

The issue is not my convenience.

The issue is Calyx's overall quality of life.

---

# Decision Hierarchy

1. Balanced childhood
2. Sustainable schedule
3. Local community
4. Support dance
5. Competitive opportunities

Competition should support childhood.

Childhood should not support competition.
